What is a Graph?

A graph is a non-linear data structure consisting of a set of vertices (nodes) and edges that connect pairs of vertices. Graphs are used to model relationships between objects.

Basic Terminology

  • Vertex (Node): Fundamental unit of a graph.
  • Edge: Connection between two vertices.
  • Weighted Graph: Edges have weights (cost/distance).
  • Unweighted Graph: All edges have equal weight.
  • Directed Graph (Digraph): Edges have direction.
  • Undirected Graph: Edges have no direction.
  • Degree: Number of edges connected to a vertex.
  • Path: Sequence of vertices connected by edges.
  • Cycle: A path that starts and ends at same vertex.

Types of Graphs

  • Directed & Undirected Graphs
  • Weighted & Unweighted Graphs
  • Connected & Disconnected Graphs
  • Cyclic & Acyclic Graphs (DAG)
  • Complete Graph
  • Tree (Special type of graph)

Graph Representations

  • Adjacency Matrix: 2D matrix representation.
  • Adjacency List: List of neighbors for each node.
  • Edge List: List of all edges.

Applications of Graphs

  • 🌐 Social Networks (Friends, Followers)
  • 🗺 GPS & Shortest Path (Google Maps)
  • 📡 Network Routing & Internet
  • 📦 Dependency Resolution (Package Managers)
  • 🕸 Web Crawling & Search Engines
  • 🧠 AI & Recommendation Systems
  • 🔌 Electrical Circuits
  • 🧬 Biological Networks

Why Graph Algorithms Matter?

Graph algorithms help solve real-world problems like finding shortest paths, detecting cycles, building minimum cost networks, analyzing connectivity, and optimizing routes.
